Here are the quantified rules and guidelines for resume layout and appearance found in the sources:

**Length and Spacing**
*   Restrict the length to **exactly one page** (especially for students or recent graduates), making sure it fills at least three-quarters of the page. The only exception mentioned is for federal jobs, where 2-3 pages are advantageous.
*   Set your margins to be between **0.5 inches and 1 inch** on all sides.

**Typography and Color**
*   Use a standard, readable font (such as Calibri, Times New Roman, or Arial) sized between **10 and 12 points**.
*   Make your name stand out by placing it at the top of the page in a larger, **12 to 18-point font**, which can be bolded and centered.
*   Use **100% black text** and avoid using any colored fonts.

**Structure and Bullet Points**
*   Never use paragraphs to describe your experience; always use bulleted statements.
*   Provide **2 to 6 bullet points** for each experience entry.
*   Keep each individual bullet point concise, limiting them to **1 to 2 lines in length**.
*   Position your most important and relevant information on the **top half and the left-hand side** of the document.

**ATS (Applicant Tracking System) Compatibility**
*   **Do not use headers, footers, text boxes, or graphics**, as these elements break ATS parsers and render your text unreadable. 
*   **Avoid multi-column layouts.** Research shows that about 20% of resumes use multi-column or non-linear designs, which frequently cause standard parsers to mangle the reading order and misattribute your skills to the wrong jobs.
*   Keep your formatting highly consistent throughout the document, allowing for **no more than 2 spacing or formatting errors**.

**Regarding FAANG Companies:**
The provided sources **do not contain any specific resume layout advice tailored for FAANG** (Facebook, Amazon, Apple, Netflix, Google) or similar tech giants. These companies are only mentioned in the sources as placeholder names within synthetic cover letter datasets and job matching examples.
